Overview

A strategic hybrid of Chess and Tic-Tac-Toe played on a 4×4 grid. Two teams — RED and BLU — each control 3 pieces: a Rook, a Knight, and a Pawn.

Objective

Be the first to align all 3 of your pieces in a straight line — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

① Opening Phase
  • RED places one piece anywhere on the board, then BLU places one — and so on.
  • Each player must place at least 2 pieces before they can move or capture.
② Player Actions

Once you have 2 pieces deployed, on your turn choose one:

  • Place — put your 3rd piece on any empty square.
  • Move — move an existing piece using standard Chess movement.
  • Capture — take an opponent's piece (subject to cooldown).
③ Movement Rules
  • Rook — slides any number of squares in a straight line; blocked by pieces in its path.
  • Knight — jumps in an L-shape (2 + 1 squares); can leap over other pieces.
  • Pawn — moves 1 square orthogonally (any direction); captures 1 square diagonally.
⚔ Capture & Respawn
  • A captured piece returns to its owner's lobby and can be redeployed on any future turn.
  • Respawn restriction: you cannot place a returning piece on a square that immediately completes a winning line.
  • Cooldown: after capturing, you must wait one full turn before capturing again. The ⚔ COOLDOWN indicator appears in the status bar.
🏆 First to align all 3 pieces wins!
